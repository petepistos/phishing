🔎 Red Flags in That Email

  • Urgency pressure: Phishing emails create a false sense of urgency to make you act without thinking.
  • Sender address mismatch: Always check that the email came from a legitimate domain before clicking.
  • Unexpected message: Legitimate systems usually know your name and don't send unexpected alerts.
  • Suspicious link URL: Hover over any link before clicking to verify it goes where it claims.
  • Request to click or log in: Legitimate IT departments rarely ask you to click a link to solve a security problem.

🛡 How to Protect Yourself

1
Stop and think before clicking. If an email creates urgency, pause and verify through a separate channel.
2
Verify the sender. Check the full email address, not just the display name.
3
Don't click — go directly. Navigate directly to the website rather than clicking links in email.
4
Report suspicious emails. Forward to your IT department. Never delete them without reporting.
5
When in doubt, call. Call the sender directly using a number you already know.
